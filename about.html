About Us

Inkonjane is a black owned construction company based in South Africa with its offices located in:

  • Head Office: Lusikisiki – Eastern Cape
  • Regional office: Durban – Kwa Zulu Natal

Established in 2000, Inkonjane Project Solutions cc, Trading as IPS, is a 100% black empowered and managed investment holding company with majority female equity. As the province's reputable company, Inkonjane strives to make an ongoing contribution to sustainable development and growth of various companies. Over the past eight years, the company has built itself into a significant sized private company with direct and indirect investment in the property development, logistic and construction sectors.

The company boasts a mixture of knowledge capitals with significant hands-on experience and support expertise. It combines tried and tested tools and methods, business analysis and technology with experience and innovative network.

Using methodologies and techniques leveraged through these networks, Inkonjane can roll out efficient investment solutions and services to projects and businesses of any size or scope, within the Eastern Cape region or across Southern Africa. In addition, Inkonjane possesses deep industry skills from across the spectrum of municipality and other public sectors.

BEE Policy

The Company has an ideal Minimal Internal Affirmative Procurement target of 40% per contract.

Inkonjane Project Solutions cc. have 100% BEE shareholding in the respective companies. In terms of the DTI's BBBEE scorecard, the Building Company is graded as a Level 2 contributor recognizing a procurement score of 125%. Inkonjane Project Solutions cc. is currently a Level 5 contributor thus ensuring procurement recognition of 110%.

Safety

At Inkonjane the safety of our employees is non-negotiable. We believe that working safely must be a way of life. This is demonstrated by numerous milestones being achieved on various projects.

We are committed to ensuring that our operations comply with the Occupational Health and Safety Act, the Compensation for Occupational injuries and Diseases Act, Environmental legislation.
